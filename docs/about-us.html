Our history

In the heart of Waterloo, a new dawn in packaging emerged three decades ago. Chris Bird, deeply rooted in a family lineage of packaging expertise, envisioned a different kind of venture with Packaging Too. While the legacy of his family’s passion in packaging set a sturdy foundation, Chris set out to fill a void no one else was addressing.

Packaging Too was built on two groundbreaking pillars: unparalleled flexibility and a relentless focus on customer service. Understanding the evolving needs of businesses, Chris broke the norm and introduced something revolutionary: No Minimum Order Quantity. This decision wasn't just about business; it was about understanding and valuing every customer's unique requirements, whether they needed one box or a thousand.

How to measure a box

  1. Measure the length of the box first. This is the long side of the box on the side of the longest flap.
  2. Next, turn the box 90 degrees and measure the width, which is the side with the shorter flap.
  3. Last, measure the height of the package. This will be measured with the flaps closed, from top to bottom.
